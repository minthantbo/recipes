Nan Gyi Thoke


Return to home

Nan gyi thoke is an a thoke salad dish in Burmese cuisine, made with thick round rice noodles mixed with specially prepared chicken curry and chili oil.


Ingredients

  • 800g thick rice noodles (fresh)
  • 4 boiled eggs, sliced
  • 1/4 cabbage, thinly sliced
  • 2-3 shallots, thinly sliced
  • 2-3 shallots, thinly sliced
  • 30g besan flour, toasted
  • Chilli oil
  • 1 lime, wedges
  • 1 bunch coriander, chopped

Steps

  1. Marinade the chicken thigh fillets in tumeric, sweet paprika powder and fish sauce.
  2. Prepare the salad toppings.
  3. Prepare the noodles.
  4. Toast the besan flour in a small pan on high heat, stirring until it smells fragrant and set aside.
  5. In a small bowl, place 1 tbsp of chilli powder.
  6. In a large saucepan, heat the peanut oil and sautee the onions and garlic until they start to caramelise at the edges.
  7. Add the marinated chicken and just enough water to immerse the chicken pieces to about three-quarters.
  8. Let it simmer for a further 10 minutes until cooked through and some of the oil starts to pool at the top.
  9. Place some noodles on a plate
  10. Top with a couple of spoonfuls of the chicken curry, some cabbage, onion, coriander, egg slices, a spoonful of besan flour, a spoonful of chilli oil and a dash of fish sauce.
  11. Garnish with fried onion and serve.
